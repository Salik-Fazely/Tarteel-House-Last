OUR TEACHERS

A small, carefully chosen team

Tarteel House is built on the quiet care of a small group of teachers — chosen one at a time, for the rare combination of Qur’anic depth and the patience to teach a child well.

WHAT WE LOOK FOR

What we look for in a teacher

Qualifications open the door. They are never enough on their own.

A serious relationship with the Qur’an.

Every teacher we work with has a deep grounding in Qur’anic recitation and tajwid — most through years of study with recognised scholars or institutions. We ask about their teachers, their training, and the path that brought them here. The Qur’an deserves to be taught by people who carry it with care.

The patience to teach a child.

Teaching a five-year-old who is restless is a different discipline from teaching a teenager who is ready. Our teachers are chosen for the particular calm needed for children — the willingness to repeat, to slow down, and to stay warm even when the lesson does not go as planned.

Clarity in how they teach.

Lessons should not feel improvised. Our teachers know how to build a path forward — week by week, level by level — so families can see the journey unfolding.

A presence children quietly trust.

Through a screen, a child can feel whether a teacher is truly with them. This is harder to articulate than the other three, but it is the one we hold ourselves to most strictly.

HOW MATCHING WORKS

We do the matching. You confirm in the trial.

Most parents do not want to spend an evening comparing teachers. So we don’t ask you to.

When you book a free trial, share your child’s age, current level, language preference, and anything else that matters — a quiet child, a confident child, a particular teacher gender preference, a difficult schedule. We read every booking personally and propose the teacher we believe fits best.

You meet that teacher in the trial session. If the match feels right to you and your child, you continue with them. If it doesn’t, tell us — and we will help you find a teacher who fits better. We would rather take a second try than let a family settle.

Our matching is done by a person at Tarteel House, not by software. It takes a little longer. We believe it matters.

YOUR CHOICE

You can also choose your own teacher

Some families know exactly which teacher they would like — perhaps because they recognise a teacher’s training, or because the introduction simply spoke to them.

If that’s you, mention the teacher’s name on the booking form. As long as that teacher is currently accepting new families, we will arrange the trial directly with them.

If they are fully booked, we will say so honestly — and offer the closest match from the rest of the team.

BEYOND THE LESSON

The teaching that happens around the session

AFTER EVERY SESSION

A short summary

After each lesson, your child’s teacher writes a brief note — what was covered, how your child engaged, what comes next. You stay informed without having to ask.

EVERY TEN SESSIONS

A check-in call

A 15-minute video call between you and your child’s teacher, where progress is reviewed together. This is the moment when wondering ends and knowing begins.

ALWAYS

A long view of the journey

Our teachers do not see lessons as isolated hours. They are building toward something — your child’s relationship with the Qur’an, over months and years.
