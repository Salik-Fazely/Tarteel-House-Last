TARTEEL HOUSE — CHARACTER USAGE GUIDE

Purpose
This guide explains how to use the recurring Tarteel House characters consistently across Instagram carousels, website visuals, reel covers, ads, and other brand materials.

The goal is to make Tarteel House feel:
premium, calm, warm, trustworthy, parent-friendly, modern, and spiritually respectful.


1. Core Characters

Adam — Boy Learner
Role:
Adam represents young boys learning Qur’an online with Tarteel House.

Use Adam for:
- one-to-one learning scenes
- online lesson scenes
- reading practice
- writing practice
- tajweed learning
- memorisation support
- progress and study routine posts

Visual rules:
- Keep Adam around 8–10 years old.
- Keep his short dark slightly wavy hair.
- Keep his olive-green shirt, tan pants, and beige sneakers style.
- Do not add a hat or cap.
- Keep him calm, focused, and gentle.
- Prefer side view, back view, or soft 3/4 view for privacy-safe visuals.
- Avoid strong close-up child face shots.


Maryam — Girl Learner
Role:
Maryam represents young girls learning Qur’an online with Tarteel House.

Use Maryam for:
- Arabic letters practice
- reading Qur’an
- memorisation posts
- progress posts
- calm study moments
- parent-friendly learning scenes
- online lesson scenes

Visual rules:
- Keep Maryam around 7–10 years old.
- Keep her dark shoulder-length hair.
- Keep her modest clothing style in sage, dusty rose, beige, and cream tones.
- Keep her expression calm, gentle, and thoughtful.
- Prefer side view, back view, or soft 3/4 view for privacy-safe visuals.
- Avoid strong close-up child face shots.
- Avoid making her look too fashionable, too childish, or too cartoonish.


Teacher Amina — Female Qur’an Teacher
Role:
Teacher Amina represents the caring female teachers of Tarteel House.

Use Teacher Amina for:
- teacher trust posts
- online lesson scenes
- live lesson explanation posts
- tajweed guidance
- memorisation support
- parent communication posts
- free trial invitation posts
- “real teacher, not pre-recorded videos” messages

Visual rules:
- Keep her as a young adult/adult female Qur’an teacher.
- Keep her taupe-beige hijab.
- Keep her modest beige, sage, and warm neutral clothing.
- Keep her expression warm, patient, and professional.
- She can be shown front-facing.
- She can appear on a laptop screen during online lesson scenes.
- She can be shown teaching with a headset, laptop, Qur’an, notebook, or Arabic lesson board.
- Avoid making her look too corporate, too casual, or too fashion-focused.


2. Brand Style

All character visuals should follow this style:

- Premium editorial illustration
- Calm and minimal composition
- Warm and parent-friendly
- Modern home-learning atmosphere
- Inspired by soft modern editorial illustration
- Not cartoonish
- Not childish
- Not loud
- Not cluttered
- Not stock-photo looking

The characters should feel suitable for Muslim families in Europe and the US.


3. Brand Color Palette

Use these colors as the main visual direction:

Warm Cream
Use for backgrounds and calm home interiors.

Sage Green
Use for soft backgrounds, clothing, and gentle accents.
Suggested hex: #647A62

Deep Forest Green
Use for logo, Qur’an cover, small accents, and premium details.
Suggested hex: #1F3D33

Warm Sand
Use for desks, walls, furniture, and soft background details.
Suggested hex: #E8DFC9

Clay Beige
Use for clothing, teacher hijab tones, and warm neutral details.
Suggested hex: #D9C8B1

Charcoal
Use for typography.
Suggested hex: #262626

Avoid:
- neon colors
- bright primary colors
- loud blues
- strong reds
- childish yellow
- heavy black backgrounds


4. Typography Direction

For Instagram carousel text:
- Use elegant serif-style typography for main headings.
- Use clean sans-serif typography for supporting text.
- Keep text very readable.
- Use dark charcoal for main text.
- Use deep green only for accents.

Typography should feel:
premium, calm, elegant, and clear.


5. Privacy and Safety Rules

For children:
- Prefer back view, side view, or soft 3/4 view.
- Avoid full-face close-up portraits.
- Do not make children look like real identifiable children.
- Do not show children in emotional, dramatic, or exaggerated expressions.
- Keep scenes calm, safe, and respectful.

For teachers:
- Teacher Amina can be shown more clearly because she represents the teacher character.
- Keep her professional, modest, and warm.


6. Scene Types

Scene A — Online Lesson at Home
Use for:
- live online lessons
- one-to-one learning
- free trial explanation
- real teacher message

Elements:
- Adam or Maryam seated at a desk
- laptop open
- Teacher Amina visible on screen
- Qur’an or notebook on the desk
- pencil or Arabic practice sheet
- soft home background
- plant, lamp, or simple shelf

Mood:
focused, calm, guided, and reassuring.


Scene B — Qur’an Reading Moment
Use for:
- Qur’an reading posts
- tajweed posts
- memorisation posts
- spiritual learning posts

Elements:
- Adam or Maryam reading Qur’an
- calm desk or home-learning setting
- warm light
- minimal props
- no dramatic mosque background

Mood:
peaceful, focused, respectful, and gentle.


Scene C — Arabic Letters / Writing Practice
Use for:
- beginner reading
- Arabic alphabet practice
- child progress
- learning routine posts

Elements:
- Maryam or Adam writing in a notebook
- Arabic letters practice sheet
- pencil
- Qur’an nearby
- clean desk

Mood:
encouraging, simple, focused, and child-friendly but premium.


Scene D — Teacher Guidance
Use for:
- teacher trust posts
- female teacher posts
- how lessons work
- live guidance
- tajweed support

Elements:
- Teacher Amina at desk
- laptop or headset
- Qur’an or notebook
- Arabic lesson board
- warm clean background

Mood:
patient, professional, caring, and trustworthy.


Scene E — Parent Progress Updates
Use for:
- monthly progress reports
- parent communication
- WhatsApp updates
- trust-building posts

Elements:
- simple phone or progress note
- child learning in the background
- notebook or report card
- calm parent-friendly home setting

Mood:
reassuring, organized, clear, and trustworthy.

8. Instagram Carousel Structure

Recommended carousel flow:

Slide 1:
Cover slide with brand message.

Slide 2:
Main benefit or problem/solution.

Slide 3:
Live online lesson explanation.

Slide 4:
Teacher trust / caring female teachers.

Slide 5:
Qur’an reading, tajweed, or memorisation.

Slide 6:
Parent progress updates.

Slide 7:

9. Prompt Rules for AI Generation

When generating new visuals, always include:

- Use the Tarteel House character reference.
- Keep the character visually consistent.
- Premium editorial illustration.
- Warm cream or muted sage background.
- Dark charcoal typography.
- Deep green accents.
- Calm home-learning atmosphere.
- Subtle Islamic geometric detail.
- Parent-friendly and trustworthy.
- Not cartoonish.
- Not childish.
- No loud colors.
- No clutter.
- No stock-photo look.

For Adam, always include:
- no hat or cap
- short dark wavy hair
- olive-green shirt
- tan pants
- beige sneakers
- calm focused expression

For Maryam, always include:
- dark shoulder-length hair
- modest sage, dusty rose, beige, and cream clothing
- calm thoughtful expression
- privacy-safe child view when possible

For Teacher Amina, always include:
- taupe-beige hijab
- modest beige and sage clothing
- warm professional expression
- can teach with laptop, headset, Qur’an, notebook, or Arabic board


10. Things to Avoid

Avoid:
- random new character designs
- inconsistent clothing colors
- cartoon mascot style
- anime style
- Pixar-style characters
- exaggerated big eyes
- loud school colors
- stock-photo families
- crowded classroom scenes
- too many children in one image
- dark dramatic religious backgrounds
- excessive mosque decorations
- children looking directly into camera in close-up
- teacher looking too corporate or too casual
- fake or messy Arabic text as the main focus
- using AI-generated text as final text in the image


11. Recommended Workflow

Step 1:
Generate or select the character image.

Step 2:
Generate the slide background/illustration with AI.

Step 3:
Upload the image to Canva.

Step 4:
Add the final text manually in Canva.

Step 5:
Export the final Instagram slide as PNG.

Step 6:
Save the final design in:
assets/Social/Instagram/Carousels/

13. Brand Feeling Checklist

Before publishing any visual, check:

Does it feel calm?
Does it feel premium?
Does it feel trustworthy?
Does it feel warm for parents?
Does it look consistent with Tarteel House?
Are the colors soft and elegant?
Is the text readable?
Is the child privacy-safe?
Does it avoid cartoonish style?
Does it clearly support the message?

If the answer is yes, the visual fits Tarteel House.
