OUR TEACHERS

A small, carefully chosen team

Tarteel House is built on the quiet care of a small group of female Qur’an teachers who teach girls and boys aged 5 to 16 online — chosen one at a time for Qur’anic depth and the patience to teach a child well.

WHAT WE LOOK FOR

What we look for in a teacher

Qualifications open the door. They are never enough on their own.

A serious relationship with the Qur’an.

Every teacher we work with has a deep grounding in Qur’anic recitation and tajwid — most through years of study with recognised scholars or institutions. We ask about their teachers, their training, and the path that brought them here. The Qur’an deserves to be taught by people who carry it with care.

The patience to teach a child.

Teaching a five-year-old who is restless is a different discipline from teaching a sixteen-year-old who is ready. Our teachers are chosen for the particular calm needed for children — the willingness to repeat, to slow down, and to stay warm even when the lesson does not go as planned.

Clarity in how they teach.

Lessons should not feel improvised. Our teachers know how to build a path forward — week by week, level by level — so families can see the journey unfolding.

A presence children quietly trust.

Through a screen, a child can feel whether a teacher is truly with them. This is harder to articulate than the other three, but it is the one we hold ourselves to most strictly.

HOW MATCHING WORKS

We do the matching. You confirm in the trial.

Most parents do not want to spend an evening comparing teachers. So we don’t ask you to.

When you book a free trial, share your child’s language, level, learning needs, scheduling availability, and any teacher preference. We read every booking personally and propose a suitable teacher.

You meet that teacher in the trial session. If your family is not satisfied with the teaching approach, you may request a teacher change. We will consider the request and propose another suitable teacher subject to availability.

Our matching is done by a person at Tarteel House, not by software. It takes a little longer. We believe it matters.

YOUR PREFERENCE

You can express a teacher preference

Parents may express a preference, but they do not directly select or reserve a specific teacher.

Tarteel House considers the preference alongside the child’s language, level, learning needs, and scheduling availability before proposing a suitable match.

A specific teacher or schedule cannot be guaranteed. If a preferred teacher is unavailable, we will propose another suitable teacher subject to availability.

BEYOND THE LESSON

The teaching that happens around the session

EVERY TWO MONTHS

A progress review

Every two months, we invite parents to a 15-minute progress review. If a parent is unavailable or does not want a meeting, we send a visual progress report through WhatsApp instead.

WHEN A STAGE IS COMPLETE

A meaningful certificate

A certificate is issued only after at least 30 lessons, completion of an identifiable learning stage, and the teacher’s confirmation that the stage is complete.

ALWAYS

A long view of the journey

Our teachers do not see lessons as isolated hours. They are building toward something — your child’s relationship with the Qur’an, over months and years.
